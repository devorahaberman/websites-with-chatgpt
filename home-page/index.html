This week’s symptom

Procrastination

Procrastination in ADHD often isn’t laziness—it’s a friction point where overwhelm, unclear steps, perfectionism, or low dopamine collide. Tasks can feel impossible to start even when you *want* to begin. This delay can create stress spirals, guilt, and last-minute rushes, but with the right structures, you can lower the activation energy and make starting easier.

Tools to Try

  • The “10-Minute Start”: Set a timer and agree to stop after 10 minutes.
  • Body-doubling (in person or virtual).
  • Breaking tasks into ultra-tiny steps.
  • Using a dopamine anchor such as music or a reward.
  • External reminders: visual cues, alarms, sticky notes.
  • The “Worst First” or “Easiest First” tactic depending on energy.
